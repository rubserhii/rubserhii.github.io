Not suspension work — but the replacement tail light assembly had arrived
and I wanted to get it fitted while I had the energy.

## Why the tail lights

The right lens had a hairline crack along the lower register. Not structural,
not a safety issue, but the kind of thing that gets worse. Water gets in,
the housing oxidises, and eventually you're looking at a much bigger job.

Sourced a clean used unit from a wrecker. Matching tail lights are not
hard to find on Del Sols — there's still a decent community of people
parting these out.

## What I found

Pulling the assemblies revealed surface oxidation on the housing mount
points on both sides. Nothing structural, but worth treating before
reinstallation. Hit them with rust converter and gave it 24 hours.

The wiring harness on both sides is in good shape — original connectors,
no corrosion, no previous butchery. That's a good sign for everything
else I'll find back there.

## Result

Clean swap. The replacement unit fits perfectly and the lenses match.
It's a small thing but the rear of the car looks intentional now
rather than worn.

**Time:** ~2 hours including cleanup and treatment.
