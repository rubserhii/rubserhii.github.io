Before touching anything, I wanted to document where the car actually is.
It's easy to forget the starting point once work is underway.

## Suspension — current state

The original struts are still in. They're tired — the fronts have some play
and the rears feel dead. Not catastrophically bad, but clearly worn.

The bushings are the bigger story. Control arm bushings are cracked and
compressed on both sides. The sway bar end links have enough slop to feel
it at low speed.

**Plan:** Full suspension refresh. Coilovers rather than OEM replacement —
the car is already sitting well on the RAYS wheels and I want to be able to
dial height properly once the interior work begins.

## Subframe

Surprisingly solid. Some surface rust on the underside that needs treating
before anything goes back up, but no structural concerns. This was the
thing I was most worried about — old Honda, Vancouver winters — but it's
held up.

## What's next

Sourcing the coilovers. I'll document the decision and why once I've landed
on a choice. Then fitment begins.

> The car doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be right.
